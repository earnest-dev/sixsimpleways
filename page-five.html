Five. Stretch your creative muscles with made up briefs.

What's the idea?

Run ideas sessions with made up briefs for brands you love (or are completely outside of your sector) and let your innovative, creative minds run wild - at Earnest we call these ‘Brief Encounters’.

Why is it so important?

Good ideas and inspiration for innovation often comes from strange places - and when trying to come up with ideas for your own business it’s easy to get stuck.

Made up briefs enable you to get thinking outside of your normal world and outside of your comfort zone - which gets you thinking about tools, techniques and channels you might never had thought of before but could actually be applicable to your business.

How do you get started?

First, pick yourself a brand and make up a really interesting brief. There are three ways we would suggest doing this - either come up with a big problem to get your teeth stuck into (this could be a charity), a brief that relates to your company (i.e how would one of your competitors tackle a brief) or something that everyone in the room can relate to (for example in the past we looked at how Honest Burger could reposition itself a after night takeaway chain.)

Like with the ideas sprints, make sure to get a group of the right people in a room and split up into groups to tackle the brief separately and come back to pitch the ideas to each other. Unlike a traditional ideas sprint, this should feel like fun and a more relaxed process so don’t be afraid to add your own twist to it.

Finally make sure those ideas don’t go to waste - mock them up or create a prototype of the best ideas and have a think about how they potentially could be used in your own marketing programmes.
